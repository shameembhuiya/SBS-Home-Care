# SBS Home Care — Site Structure & SEO Content Strategy

This document covers everything from the redesign brief that lives outside the homepage build: sitemap, per-service SEO content, meta data, schema recommendations, and ranking/conversion guidance. The homepage itself has been built and delivered separately as `index.html` / `styles.css`.

---

## 1. Recommended Site Structure (Sitemap)

```
/ (Home)
/services/
  /services/personal-care/
  /services/companion-care/
  /services/elderly-care/
  /services/dementia-care/
  /services/alzheimers-care/
  /services/respite-care/
  /services/live-in-care/
  /services/post-hospital-care/
  /services/disability-support/
  /services/palliative-care/
/service-areas/
  /service-areas/bronx/
  /service-areas/manhattan/
  /service-areas/brooklyn/
  /service-areas/queens/
  /service-areas/westchester/
  /service-areas/fairfield-county-ct/
  /service-areas/new-haven-county-ct/
/about-us/
/careers/
/testimonials/
/resources/ (blog index)
  /resources/[article-slug]/
/contact/
/faq/
```

**Why this structure:** Each service gets its own URL so it can rank independently for its own keyword cluster (e.g., "dementia care Bronx" vs. "live-in care Westchester"). Service-area pages let you rank for "home care near me"-style local searches without diluting the main service pages. This is standard practice for home care SEO — it's how the larger agencies capture long-tail local search volume.

## 4. Per-Service SEO Content

Each service below follows the same template: SEO title/description (see table above), keyword-rich intro, 3 benefits, a short FAQ, and a CTA. Use this as the copy source when building out each `/services/[slug]/` page.

### Personal Care
**Primary keywords:** personal care services, in-home personal care, help with bathing and dressing seniors

> Personal care from SBS Home Care means real, hands-on help with the parts of the day that have become difficult — bathing, dressing, grooming, toileting, and getting around safely. Our caregivers are trained to assist with patience and respect, so your loved one keeps their dignity while staying safe at home.

**Benefits:** Reduces fall risk during transfers and bathing · Keeps a consistent daily routine · Frees up family caregivers for quality time instead of physical tasks

**FAQ:**
- *Can personal care be combined with companion care?* Yes — most clients blend both into one visit.
- *How many hours of personal care can I schedule?* From a few hours a week up to full-time live-in support.

**CTA:** Schedule a free personal care assessment today.

## 5. Local SEO Content Themes

Build short, locally-flavored landing pages or sections around these search terms — each should reference the specific neighborhood/county by name at least 3–4 times naturally:

- "Home care near me" → service-area pages
- "Elderly care services in [Bronx / Westchester / Fairfield County]"
- "In-home care assistance [city name]"
- "Senior care services near [landmark or neighborhood]"
- "Trusted home care agency [county name]"

Each service-area page should include: a short intro naming the area, which services are available there, a local testimonial if possible, and the same contact CTA used sitewide.

## 6. Schema Markup Recommendations

Already implemented on the homepage:
- `HomeHealthCareService` (LocalBusiness subtype) with NAP, areaServed, and aggregateRating
- `FAQPage` schema for the homepage FAQ accordion

**Recommended for additional pages:**
- Each service page → `Service` schema with `serviceType`, `provider`, and `areaServed`
- Each blog article → `Article` or `BlogPosting` schema with `author`, `datePublished`, `image`
- Service-area pages → reuse `HomeHealthCareService` with `areaServed` scoped to that specific city/county
- Add `BreadcrumbList` schema once nested pages exist (Home > Services > Dementia Care)

## 8. Recommendations to Maximize Rankings & Lead Generation

1. **Claim and complete a Google Business Profile** with the exact NAP (name/address/phone) used in the schema markup — this matters more for local home care search than almost anything else.
2. **Build location pages, not just a coverage list.** A page per major service area outperforms a single page that lists 8 towns.
3. **Get review velocity going.** Add a simple post-visit text/email asking for a Google review — aggregate rating count is a real local-ranking factor.
4. **Internal-link service pages to each other** (e.g., the Dementia Care page should link to Respite Care, since family caregivers often need both).
5. **Track call conversions**, not just form fills — most home care leads call directly from search or the sticky mobile CTA. Consider call tracking numbers per channel.
6. **Keep page speed lean** — avoid auto-playing background video on mobile (the slider in the new homepage uses static images for this reason); compress all photography to WebP where possible.
7. **Add structured FAQ content to every service page**, not just the homepage — FAQ schema is one of the more reliable ways to win extra SERP real estate for a site this size.
